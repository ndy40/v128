Photograph of house in which Bai Bureh was kept captive in Ascension Town in 1898.

To complement the recently discovered photograph of Bai Bureh taken by Lt Greer of the West India Regiment while being held captive in Freetown in 1898, here is a photograph held in the Sierra Leone National Archives of the house in Ascension Town where he was held under guard until he was deported to the Gold Coast (Ghana).

According to a note on the photograph, the Sherbro chief Kpana Lewis and the Mende chief Nyagua were held here under house arrest prior to deportation. There is also a suggestion that the Asantehene Prempeh I was held here from 1897, prior to being exiled to the Seyschelles (where he arrived in September 1900). The building no longer exists, but a note states that it was on a plot beside the 'Mahmoud Ahmed Building' - does anyone know what street this is on or have a photograph of the Mahmoud Ahmed Building?

Judging from the style of thatching, the building photographed does seem to be the one in the background of the Greer image.
